Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is a Corporate Services Provider (CSP)?

A: A CSP is a company that helps businesses with administrative, legal, and operational tasks, such as company formation, license renewal, and a range of other corporate-related services. We act as your expert guide to navigate the business landscape.

Q: How long does it take to set up a new company in Dubai?

The timeline can vary depending on the business activity, jurisdiction (Mainland or Free Zone), and government approvals. We work efficiently to fast-track the process and will give you a clear, estimated timeline during your initial consultation.

Q: What documents are required to start a company with you?

The primary documents usually include a passport copy, UAE residency visa (if applicable), and an Emirates ID. Specific requirements may vary based on your business activity and legal structure. We will provide a detailed checklist during our consultation.

Q: Do you assist with visa applications and processing?

Yes, we provide comprehensive visa services for company owners and employees. Our services cover everything from initial application and medical tests to Emirates ID processing, making the process smooth and efficient.

Q: How can I get a new telecom connection through you?

You can simply contact us with your requirements for a mobile or internet plan. We will handle the entire process, from selecting the best plan from Etisalat or Du to completing the application and arranging for installation, saving you time and effort.

Q: Which telecom providers do you work with?

We work with both Etisalat and Du, the two primary telecom providers in the UAE, to offer a full range of mobile, landline, and high-speed fibre optic services.

Q: Can you help me with a specific technical issue with my internet?

While we are not a technical support team, we act as a liaison to Etisalat and Du's customer service departments. We can help you communicate your issue effectively and follow up to ensure it is resolved quickly.

Q: Do I need a Local Service Agent (LSA)?

If you are setting up a Sole Establishment in the UAE as a non-GCC national, a Local Service Agent (LSA) is required by law. The LSAâ€™s role is to assist with government department liaison and is not involved in the company's operations or finances.

Q: Do you help with opening a business bank account?

Yes, we can guide you through the process of opening a business bank account. We will provide you with a list of required documents and connect you with the right banking professionals to simplify the application process.

Q: How much do your services cost?

Our service fees are competitive and transparent. The final cost depends on the specific services you require. We offer a free, no-obligation consultation to understand your needs and provide a clear, tailored quote upfront.
